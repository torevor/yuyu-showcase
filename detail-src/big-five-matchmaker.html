This is not a lookup table. It decomposes both you and each thinker into one shared attribute space — thirty disposition facets, the problem you bring, the competencies a text demands — and crosses them. The result is a ranked set, each with a concrete, sometimes hostile reason. The cards below are 44 genuine outputs of that computation; nothing on them was written by hand.

How it works · five capabilities

Temperament resonance

A signed cosine over 30 disposition facets — need-for-cognition, tolerance-for-ambiguity, method-formality, the registers you want handed to you. This is fit my mind: how close a thinker's demands sit to your dispositions.

see a reading that shows this →

Aptness

A separate score for whether a thinker is any good on the problem you actually brought — which can flatly contradict temperament. The matcher shows both numbers rather than blending them into one flattering average.

see a reading that shows this →

The admissibility gate

Competency is a gate, not a distance. If you cannot yet read the notation a mode requires, it is refused — you are not a partial match for a text you cannot open. The gap is reported as a curriculum, never folded into the score.

see a reading that shows this →

Productive friction

High aptness + high admissibility + deliberate dispositional distance. Under the break-me stance the instrument stops flattering you and hands you the thinker who will fight you — because that is where the work is.

see a reading that shows this →

It shows its work

Every reason traces to a number on the page, and every stipulated reading carries its own falsifier. The philosopher vectors are labelled our interpretations, never facts — an evidential marker no assertion here is constructible without.

Try it yourself

The matchmaker is a deterministic Python program — same answers in, same ranking and reason out, no model in the loop. Answer the questionnaire and it writes you a card like the ones above:

git clone https://github.com/torevor/big-five-to-philosopher-matchmaker
cd big-five-to-philosopher-matchmaker/slice

# answer interactively, and write a viewable card:
python session.py --interactive --stance stretch --html my-reading.html

# or reproduce any card in the gallery from its committed answer-file:
python session.py ../showcase/readings/<id>.answers.json --stance stretch --html card.html

Stances: stretch (fit my mind) · comfort (what it hands me) · break (fight me).

A research instrument, not an oracle. The philosopher vectors are stipulated interpretations — our reading of what each mode rewards or presupposes — labelled as such, never asserted as fact, each carrying a falsifier. Every number on every card was computed by match.py; every sentence of every reason traces to one of those numbers. The readers are synthetic demo profiles; the outputs are real.
