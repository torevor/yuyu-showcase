What it does

Determinism wall

127 span-anchored detectors across 14 families emit content-hashed machine facts; identical input yields a byte-identical result.

Thinking-machine bridge

Formalizes a passage into span-anchored logic, gates faithfulness verbatim, proves consistency by SAT+SMT cross-solver consensus.

Educational sidecar

A prerequisite ladder from primitive concepts up to the exact idea a finding turns on, each rung Wikipedia-grounded and provenance-graded.

▸ ops · The app opens and browses fine on its own (pre-analysed texts, the learn ladder). Live 'paste your own text and scan' calls the uvicorn engine on the box — if a fresh scan just spins, the API is off.

Paste in any piece of writing and the engine reads it the way a careful, literal analyst would — surfacing, span by span, every place a reader could reasonably be misled. Its foundation is a hard determinism wall: 127 deterministic detectors emit reproducible, content-hashed machine facts about the text, while a strictly separate, always-graded language-model layer renders interpretation on top. Facts and judgments are never merged.

Its newest capability is a thinking-machine bridge that carries a claim from messy prose into formal logic and back — a span-anchored logical form, a deterministic faithfulness gate that requires every proposition to slice back out of the source verbatim, then consistency proven by cross-solver consensus between an independent SAT and SMT encoding.
